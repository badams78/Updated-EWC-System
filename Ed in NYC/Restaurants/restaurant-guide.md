# NYC Restaurant Guide - January 2026

## NYC WINTER OUTING RESTAURANT WEEK
**January 20 - February 12, 2026**

Your trip coincides with NYC's Winter Outing! Many restaurants offer prix-fixe deals:
- Lunch: ~$30
- Dinner: ~$45-60
- **Website:** nycgo.com/restaurantweek

---

## HOT NEW RESTAURANTS (2025 Openings)

### Bánh Anh Em ⭐ MICHELIN Bib Gourmand
**Vietnamese**
- A sensation from day one with lines out the door
- Warming bowls of phở, bouncy bánh cuốn, freshly baked bánh mì
- Nearly everything made in-house, including crackly baguettes
- Chef Nhu Ton and restaurateur John Nguyen

### Kabawa
**Caribbean - Momofuku Group**
- Momofuku's first new NYC restaurant in years
- East Village location
- Chef Paul Carmichael showcases Barbadian heritage
- Try: Jerk duck sausage, braised goat with spicy scallop creole

### Adda
**Indian**
- Famous for tableside butter chicken experience
- New East Village location
- Creative, honest cooking from the original Queens storefront
- Warm, welcoming atmosphere

### Huso
**Caviar & Fine Dining - Tribeca**
- From Top Chef champion Buddha Lo
- Luxurious tasting menu celebrating the art of caviar
- Expanded from intimate UES café to downtown dining room

### Narkara
**Northern Thai**
- Address: 5 E. 17th Street
- Vibrant Northern Thai flavors
- Stunning bamboo installation
- Chef Sakdiphat Mokkasak (16 years in NYC Thai scene)

## CLASSIC NYC RESTAURANTS

### Fine Dining

**Le Bernardin**
- Legendary French seafood
- 3 Michelin stars
- Reserve well in advance

**Eleven Madison Park**
- Plant-based tasting menu
- 3 Michelin stars
- Unforgettable experience

**Per Se**
- Thomas Keller's NYC outpost
- 3 Michelin stars
- Columbus Circle views

### Iconic NYC Experiences

**Katz's Delicatessen**
- Address: 205 East Houston Street
- Famous pastrami sandwiches since 1888
- Cash only, worth the wait
- "I'll have what she's having" (When Harry Met Sally)

**Joe's Pizza**
- Multiple locations
- Classic NY slice
- Perfect late-night bite

**Russ & Daughters**
- Address: 179 East Houston Street
- Bagels, lox, and appetizing since 1914
- Try the classic bagel with cream cheese and nova

**Peter Luger Steakhouse**
- Address: 178 Broadway, Brooklyn
- NYC's most famous steakhouse since 1887
- Cash only, reservations essential

## BY NEIGHBORHOOD

### Midtown (Near Theaters)
- **Carmine's** - Family-style Italian, huge portions
- **Becco** - Italian with unlimited pasta deal
- **Joe Allen** - Classic theater district spot
- **Junior's** - Famous cheesecake (Times Square location)

### Greenwich Village / West Village
- **Joe's Pizza** - Classic slice
- **John's of Bleecker Street** - Coal-fired pizza
- **The Spotted Pig** - Gastropub (if reopened)
- **Via Carota** - Italian, hard to get into but worth it

### SoHo / Little Italy / Chinatown
- **Lombardi's** - America's first pizzeria
- **Nom Wah Tea Parlor** - Dim sum since 1920
- **Joe's Shanghai** - Soup dumplings
- **Tacombi** - Casual Mexican

### Upper West Side (Near Museums)
- **Barney Greengrass** - "Sturgeon King" - Jewish deli since 1908
- **Jacob's Pickles** - Southern comfort food, great biscuits
- **Peacefood Cafe** - Vegan-friendly

### Brooklyn
- **Peter Luger** - Steakhouse legend
- **Juliana's Pizza** - Coal-fired, by original Grimaldi's owner
- **Di Fara Pizza** - Worth the trek (cash only)
